# Trade Example: COIN

> [!summary]
> COIN (Coinbase) was focus-listed by Jeff in late January 2024 before it ran +110% from early February 2024. Jeff shared a detailed write-up of the idea, demonstrating how his focus list process identifies crypto-proxy equities with high [[adr-percent|ADR%]] as alternatives to direct crypto exposure or ETFs.

## Setup & Entry

Jeff wrote a detailed public analysis of the COIN idea in late January 2024, outlining why it qualified for his [[focus-list]]:

> "A detailed write up on focus listed COIN idea in late January 2024, before it ran +110% from early February 2024"
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 4]]

The COIN idea exemplifies several of Jeff's focus list criteria:

- **High ADR%**: As a crypto proxy, COIN offered the high [[adr-percent|average daily range]] that Jeff requires for meaningful position sizing. He consistently emphasizes that higher ADR% securities allow for tighter risk with the same capital allocation.
- **Relative Strength first, setup second**: Jeff's rule is to prioritize relative strength before evaluating the chart setup. COIN demonstrated strong [[vars-relative-strength|VARS]] relative to the broader market at the time.
- **Thematic strength**: Crypto was showing sector momentum heading into 2024, and COIN served as the most liquid equity proxy for crypto exposure.

Jeff's focus list process -- screening, then upgrading through watchlist management to an actionable idea -- is fully documented in his [COIN write-up thread](https://x.com/jfsrev/status/1751903862225776941).

## Trade Management

The source material does not detail Jeff's specific stop placement or scaling approach on this trade. However, given that COIN is a high-ADR% stock, Jeff's standard framework would apply:

- **[[3-stop-strategy]]** for initial risk management
- **[[t-plus-3-framework]]** for evaluating whether to hold past the first three days
- **[[atr-extension-from-50ma]]** for identifying when the stock becomes extended and warrants partial profit-taking

Jeff also notes the ETF alternative consideration. For crypto exposure, he tracks liquid ETFs like IBIT (Bitcoin ETF) and notes key caveats:

> "Key nuances when trading US listed ETFs that track futures with 24-hour underlying markets. eg. GLD (XAUUSD), SPXL (ES), IBIT (BTCUSD), SOLT (SOLUSD)"
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 4]]

> "Another caveat on executing US listed crypto ETFs on Friday session"
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 4]]

This suggests Jeff weighed COIN (the equity) against IBIT (the ETF) and chose the individual stock for its superior ADR% characteristics.

## Exit & Result

The stock ran **+110% from early February 2024** after Jeff's late January focus list entry. Specific exit details are not documented in the source material, but Jeff's systematic approach using [[sell-rules]] and [[atr-extension-from-50ma]] would have governed profit-taking.

## Key Lessons

1. **Crypto proxy via equities**: When a thematic trend is strong (crypto in early 2024), Jeff looks for the highest-quality equity proxy rather than trading the underlying asset or a lower-ADR% ETF.
2. **ADR% drives position sizing efficiency**: High-ADR% securities like COIN allow Jeff to achieve meaningful returns with appropriate position sizes while maintaining tight risk parameters.
3. **Focus list timing matters**: The detailed write-up in late January -- before the move began in February -- demonstrates that Jeff's screening and focus list process identifies ideas before the major move, not after.
4. **ETF vs. equity decision**: Jeff considers leveraged ETFs as alternatives (he notes Qullamaggie always chooses the leveraged ETF for higher ADR%), but for COIN the individual equity provided the better opportunity.
