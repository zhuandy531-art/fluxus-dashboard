# Top-Down Approach

> [!summary]
> Jeff uses a 4-level top-down framework to assess market health before trading individual stocks: (1) equal-weight vs. cap-weight index comparison, (2) sub-market level investigation, (3) sector-level analysis across 5 key sectors, and (4) industry group drill-down. This is supplemented by a relative strength histogram spreadsheet, Druckenmiller's homebuilder indicator, and equal-weight ETF lists for unbiased sector reads.

## Core Concept

The top-down approach answers the question: "Where is the strength in the market right now?" Rather than starting with individual stock charts, Jeff works from the broadest level down to the narrowest, filtering at each stage. This ensures that individual trade ideas are aligned with where institutional money is actually flowing, not where it was flowing last month.

The framework has four levels, each progressively narrower:

1. **Market level**: Equal-weight index (RSP) vs. cap-weight index (SPY)
2. **Sub-market level**: Investigation into market segments beyond the headline index
3. **Sector level**: Focus on 5 key sectors for breakout opportunities
4. **Industry group level**: Drill into the specific industry groups within winning sectors

## Rules / Details

**Level 1 -- Equal-Weight vs. Cap-Weight (RSP vs. SPY):**

Comparing the equal-weight S&P 500 (RSP) to the cap-weight S&P 500 (SPY) reveals whether the rally is broad-based or driven by a handful of mega-caps. When RSP outperforms SPY, the average stock is doing well -- a healthier environment for breakout trading. When SPY leads and RSP lags, leadership is narrow, and breakout traders should be more selective.

**Level 2 -- Sub-Market Investigation:**

Beyond the headline S&P 500, Jeff investigates sub-market segments: mid-caps, small-caps, and growth vs. value. This reveals whether money is rotating into riskier segments (bullish for momentum trading) or retreating to safety.

**Level 3 -- Sector Analysis (5 Key Sectors):**

Jeff identifies 5 key sectors that historically produce the best breakout opportunities. Focusing on these sectors narrows the universe and increases the probability that watchlist names are in structurally favorable groups.

**Level 4 -- Industry Group Drill-Down:**

Within the strongest sectors, Jeff drills into industry groups. This is where the most actionable information lives. A strong sector can have weak industry groups within it, and the strongest stocks almost always come from the strongest industry groups.

**Druckenmiller's Homebuilder Indicator:**

Jeff references Stanley Druckenmiller's use of homebuilders as a leading economic indicator. Homebuilder stocks tend to lead broader market moves because they are sensitive to interest rates, consumer confidence, and economic cycles. Monitoring homebuilder strength or weakness provides an early signal about the direction of the broader economy and market.

**Relative Strength Histogram Spreadsheet:**

Jeff shared a tutorial for creating a free relative strength histogram in a spreadsheet (both stocks and ETFs) that updates daily after close. This gives a visual, quantitative view of where relative strength is concentrated across the market.

**Equal-Weight ETF List:**

Jeff maintains and shares a list of equal-weight ETFs for sector analysis. Equal-weight ETFs eliminate the distortion caused by mega-cap stocks within a sector, giving a truer read on sector health. He also shares a full ETF list including cap-weighted versions for comparison.

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff's top-down process runs during his post-market and pre-market routine:

1. He compares RSP vs. SPY to establish the broad market character
2. He checks sub-market segments for rotation signals
3. He reviews his 5 key sectors for emerging or continuing strength
4. He drills into industry groups within leading sectors to find the specific areas producing the best setups
5. He cross-references with his RS histogram spreadsheet for a quantitative view
6. Stocks that pass his screeners are validated against this top-down context -- he prioritizes names in leading sectors and industry groups

This top-down filter means Jeff's focus list is always tilted toward the areas of the market with the most institutional support, improving the base rate for his breakout entries.
