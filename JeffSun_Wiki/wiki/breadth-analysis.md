# Breadth Analysis

> [!summary]
> Jeff uses market breadth as an objective layer of situational awareness. His primary tools include Qullamaggie's breadth indicator, TradingView's end-of-day breadth panel with dedicated breadth symbols, MMTW (% of stocks above the 20-MA), and the NH/NL (New Highs minus New Lows, 52-week level). These give a bird's-eye view of market health that price indexes alone cannot provide.

## Core Concept

Breadth measures how many stocks are participating in a market move. A rally on narrow breadth -- where indexes rise but few stocks participate -- is fragile. A broad rally where many stocks are above key moving averages and making new highs is structurally sound. Jeff treats breadth as one of the most straightforward objective inputs for situational awareness, sitting alongside ATR% extension and top-down sector analysis.

The distinction matters because price indexes (especially cap-weighted ones like SPY) can mask underlying weakness. A handful of mega-cap stocks can drag the index higher while the average stock deteriorates. Breadth cuts through this distortion.

## Rules / Details

**Qullamaggie's breadth indicator:**

Jeff calls this "the most straightforward breadth indicator." It provides a simple read on whether the broad market is healthy enough to support breakout trading. Jeff used this indicator to highlight potential further weakness on March 21, 2025, and then shorted the market on March 28, 2025, based on the simple MA principle it revealed.

**TradingView End-of-Day Breadth Panel:**

Jeff maintains a TradingView breadth panel that he checks at end of day. This uses TradingView's built-in breadth symbols for a bird's-eye view of market internals. He has shared how he uses these symbols to quickly assess market health without needing to check hundreds of individual charts.

**MMTW -- % of Stocks Above 20-MA:**

MMTW measures what percentage of stocks in a given universe are trading above their 20-day moving average. This is a direct measure of short-term market health. When MMTW is high, the broad market is participating in the rally. When it drops, breadth is deteriorating even if the index has not yet fallen. Jeff uses MMTW as an example of how breadth symbols provide actionable context.

**NH/NL -- New Highs Minus New Lows (52-Week):**

The short-term New Highs minus New Lows indicator on a 52-week basis measures net new highs across the market. Positive and expanding readings indicate a healthy market where many stocks are breaking to new highs. Negative readings signal broad deterioration. Jeff uses TradingView's built-in NH/NL data for this.

**March 2025 -- Breadth confirming the market was not as oversold as expected:**

In March 2025, breadth data confirmed that the market was not as oversold as many expected. While the index had pulled back, breadth indicators showed that participation had not collapsed to levels typically seen at meaningful bottoms. This breadth read supported Jeff's decision to highlight further weakness on March 21 and take short positions on March 28.

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff's breadth workflow fits into his broader situational awareness process:

1. **End of day**: He reviews the TradingView breadth panel for a quick snapshot of market internals
2. **Qullamaggie's indicator**: Provides a simple directional read -- is breadth expanding or contracting?
3. **MMTW check**: Gauges short-term participation. A low reading combined with index weakness may signal a genuine oversold condition; a low reading with a still-elevated index signals divergence
4. **NH/NL**: Confirms whether the market is producing new leaders (new highs) or hemorrhaging them (new lows dominating)
5. **Decision framework**: Breadth feeds directly into his position frequency decisions. Healthy breadth supports taking new long positions. Deteriorating breadth triggers caution on new longs and opens the door for short exposure via inverse ETFs

The March 2025 sequence is a clean example: breadth warned of weakness before the index fully broke down, Jeff flagged it publicly, and then executed his biggest short positions of the year.
