# Daily Workflow

> [!summary]
> Jeff Sun's post-market daily process follows a strict five-stage pipeline: Screening, Watchlist Management, Focus List Preparation, Situational Awareness from qualitative market reading, and Portfolio Stops Management. Consistency in this routine -- not intelligence or special talent -- is what produces super trades over time.

## Core Concept

> "Super trades are made not because you are special or intelligent, it is because you turned up & acted in a consistent manner."

The daily workflow is designed to be **time-efficient, manageable, and sustainable for long-term trading longevity**. It cannot be exhausting. The importance of a daily routine lies in its repeatability -- Jeff emphasizes that if you are in tune with your process, you will not be sidelined at the wrong time.

## The Five-Stage Post-Market Flow

### 1. Screening

Jeff runs **14 post-market screeners** across Finviz and TradingView, shifting results into dedicated watchlists organized by purpose. Screening only builds a generic watchlist -- it is the raw material, not the finished product. See [[watchlist-management]] for what comes next.

### 2. Watchlist Management

Actionable ideas only emerge after active watchlist management. This includes segmenting stocks into rotating sets, maintaining a "Back Watchlist" for calibrating screeners, and aggressively updating during volatile markets. See [[watchlist-management]].

### 3. Focus List Preparation

The watchlist is filtered down to a curated Focus List of strictly A-rated ideas. Relative Strength comes first, setup second. Every stock must show some form of Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP) in price, or both price and volume. See [[focus-list]].

### 4. Situational Awareness

Jeff reads the market qualitatively using breadth indicators, the top-down approach (market > sector > industry group), ATR% extension signals on indices, and New Highs/New Lows data. This situational awareness can be objective -- it determines whether to be aggressive or cautious. He also recommends annotating every trade (win or loss) on the index chart to evaluate your own awareness over time.

### 5. Portfolio Stops Management

If positions are open, stops are reviewed and adjusted as needed. When a durable portfolio is already established (beyond T+3), the priority shifts from new entries to adding onto existing positions that have proven to be leaders.

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff has shared that this daily routine takes dedicated hours of post-market work. The commitment scales with timeframe: **"The lower the timeframe, the greater the commitment it demands from you."** As a swing trader, Jeff's process is centered on post-market preparation rather than live-market screen time.

He strongly recommends maintaining a **daily fitness routine for mental clarity**, personally completing his before the market opens. The physical routine complements the trading routine -- both contribute to sustainable performance.

During volatile market periods, the workflow intensifies. The Aug 22-26, 2025 sequence demonstrated how rapidly the Focus List can evolve when conditions shift, requiring the trader to stay highly active with screening and watchlist updates. High-volatility environments should never be days spent in cash -- they demand knowing when to hunt.

## Key Principle

The entire workflow is a funnel: 14 screeners produce a broad watchlist, active management narrows it, and strict criteria compress it into a handful of A-rated Focus List ideas. By the time a stock reaches execution, it has survived every filter. The edge comes not from any single step but from executing this pipeline consistently, day after day.
