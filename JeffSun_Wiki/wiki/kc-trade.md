# Trade Example: KC

> [!summary]
> Jeff's most-cited trade: KC gained +180% in 6 weeks during 2024. It serves as the blueprint for his entire system -- from screening through an extended-base screener, to RVOL-confirmed breakout entry, to systematic profit-taking guided by ATR% extension from the 50-MA. Jeff uses this trade repeatedly to illustrate how each component of his process connects.

## Setup & Entry

KC was identified through Jeff's screening process for **extended bases and prolonged consolidations**, specifically Cup & Handle patterns. This trade directly inspired one of his 14 post-market screeners (Screener #3), which he describes as:

> "My 2024 +180% KC trade inspired screener - Extended bases and prolonged consolidations, such as Cup & Handle patterns."
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 3]]

The key entry confirmation came from [[rvol-based-entry|RVOL]]. Jeff emphasizes that strong volume run-rate was the first proof of strong buying:

> "Using RVOL for entry confirmation: Strong volume run-rate as first proof of strong buying for exceptional short term return (eg. my +180% KC trade in 6 weeks)"
> — [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1946777562648383700|Tweet]]

Jeff provided a detailed breakdown of the trade covering screening, [[watchlist-management]], entry day insights, and systematic risk management for holding the position ([full thread](https://x.com/jfsrev/status/1864933928924254327)).

The screening-to-focus-list upgrade followed Jeff's standard process: the stock first appeared in the extended-base screener, was added to a watchlist, then upgraded to the [[focus-list]] once it showed a [[vars-relative-strength|volatility contraction pattern (VCP)]] in price and volume, signaling it was ready to break out.

## Trade Management

Once entered, the trade was managed through Jeff's systematic framework:

- **3-Stop Strategy**: Initial risk was structured using the [[3-stop-strategy]], keeping downside capped at approximately -0.67R.
- **T+3 Framework**: After surviving the [[t-plus-3-framework|T+3]] evaluation window, the position was held with trailing stops, allowing the trend to develop.
- **Progressive sizing**: As the trade proved itself, Jeff applied [[progressive-exposure]] principles -- adding to the position as it moved in his favor, rolling risk into a proven winner.

## Exit & Result

The position was managed out using [[atr-extension-from-50ma|ATR% extension from the 50-MA]] as the sell signal framework. As KC extended further from its 50-day moving average, Jeff scaled out profits into strength -- consistent with his rule:

> "Sell Some Into Strength, Or Death By Thousand Cuts"
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 7]]

The trade returned **+180% in approximately 6 weeks**, making it one of the highest-R trades Jeff has publicly documented.

## Key Lessons

1. **RVOL as gatekeeper**: Without strong relative volume confirming the breakout, there is no entry. KC demonstrated that RVOL is the first and most critical confirmation signal.
2. **Extended bases produce outsized moves**: The prolonged consolidation (Cup & Handle) preceding KC's breakout is precisely the type of setup Jeff's Screener #3 is designed to find. Every sustainable price expansion rally is preceded by a phase of price contraction/tightening.
3. **Systematic profit-taking preserves gains**: Using ATR% extension from the 50-MA as an objective sell signal removes emotion from the exit decision.
4. **One trade can cover many losses**: Jeff's broader philosophy -- that one winning trade can cover 13+ losing streaks -- is embodied by the KC result. The math works because losses are capped at -0.67R while winners like KC run to extreme R-multiples.
5. **The entire system works as a chain**: Screening found it, watchlist management tracked it, RVOL confirmed the entry, 3-stop managed risk, T+3 validated holding, and ATR% extension guided the exit. No single component alone produced the result.
