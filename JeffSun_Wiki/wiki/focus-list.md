# Focus List

> [!summary]
> The Focus List is the final, highest-conviction output of Jeff Sun's daily workflow -- a curated set of strictly A-rated ideas that have passed through screening, watchlist management, and rigorous filtering. The cardinal rule is "Relative Strength first, setup second." Only stocks showing genuine RS leadership combined with volatility contraction qualify. Since mid-October 2025, daily Focus List sharing has been limited to Twitter/X subscribers only.

## Core Concept

> "Ultimately, I only seek to trade 'A-Rated' ideas strictly."

The Focus List sits between the broad watchlist and live execution. To upgrade a watchlisted stock to the Focus List requires it to demonstrate both relative strength leadership and some form of Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP) in price, or both price and volume. Jeff will never enter a stock with prior loose price action.

## Qualification Criteria (2023 Summary)

Jeff outlined the Focus List criteria across three threads in 2023:

**Part 1**: The initial filter -- identifying stocks that show persistent strength relative to the market and their sector peers. Names must demonstrate leadership, not just follow the trend.

**Part 2**: The setup filter -- once RS is confirmed, the stock must exhibit a constructive technical pattern. This means price tightening, volume drying up during consolidation, and a clear structure for entry.

**Part 3**: The final curation (originally called "Stalk List," later renamed Focus List) -- the handful of names that survive both filters and are ready for execution upon the right trigger.

### The Cardinal Rule: RS First, Setup Second

> "Relative Strength First, Setup Second."

This ordering is non-negotiable. A beautiful chart pattern in a stock with weak relative strength is not a Focus List candidate. Jeff notes that Qullamaggie's true focus is always relative strength as well. The importance of this filter was demonstrated on November 14, 2025, where the intraday strength and weakness of the top 10 and bottom 10 industry groups showed clear divergence from the first hour through end of day.

### Four Chart Types

Jeff shared four similar types of charts that meet Focus List criteria, highlighting key parameters within each: the stock must show RS leadership, price contraction (VCP), volume drying up, and proximity to a clean breakout level.

## How Jeff Applies It

### COIN Deep-Dive (January 2024)

Jeff published a detailed write-up on focus-listing COIN in late January 2024. The stock exhibited strong relative strength, a constructive base pattern, and volume contraction. COIN subsequently rallied +110% from early February 2024.

### WULF Thought Process

When evaluating the WGMI group (blockchain miners), Jeff's thought process led him to pick only WULF as the Focus List candidate. His reasoning isolated WULF as the stock most likely to emerge as the strongest leader within its group. WULF subsequently ran +120% in 2 months, confirming the selection.

### KC Trade (+180%)

The KC trade exemplified the full pipeline from screening through watchlist management, to Focus List entry-day insights, and systematic risk management for holding the position. This was one of Jeff's standout trades in 2024.

### The Internalization Graphic

Jeff keeps a graphic as his screensaver that outlines the **key ingredients for how a stock sets up to move exponentially higher**. It exists in two versions:
- **Simplified Version**: Core pattern of RS + VCP + breakout
- **Expanded Version**: Same concept with traditional RS indicator overlay

This graphic represents the mental model that should be internalized -- the archetype of every Focus List candidate.

## Subscriber-Only Sharing

Since mid-October 2025, Jeff has limited daily Focus List sharing to **Twitter/X subscribers only**, due to a flood of spam bots. Previously, the Focus List was shared publicly. The consolidated Focus List presentation from October 6, 2025 (with overlapping names across screeners) was among the last public examples.
