# Screener Overview

> [!summary]
> Jeff Sun runs 14 post-market screeners and 2 live-market screeners as the first stage of his daily process. Post-market screeners use both Finviz and TradingView to populate dedicated watchlists by purpose. Live-market screeners run on TradingView to monitor RVOL for execution opportunities.

## Core Concept

Screening is the entry point of Jeff's daily workflow: **Screening -> Watchlist Management -> Focus List Preparation -> Situational Awareness -> Portfolio Stops Management**. The screeners are tools that generate a generic watchlist; actionable ideas only emerge after subsequent watchlist management and focus list curation.

> "Screening only builds a generic watchlist."

> "If you're struggling to generate alpha, reassess your process or screens. The hard truth: if you're in tune, you won't be sidelined at the wrong time."

## Screening & Watchlist Management

Jeff emphasizes that screening is a commonly misunderstood skill for new traders. The screeners themselves are only the beginning; the real edge comes from disciplined watchlist management afterward. Jeff consolidates results from all screeners into a single Focus List with overlapping names across screeners, then applies further qualitative filters.

> "Actionable ideas only come after watchlist management."

A 3-minute video walkthrough demonstrates how Jeff screens all 14 screeners and transfers results into various watchlists daily.

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff runs post-market screeners after the US market close, populating purpose-specific watchlists. The Finviz mobile web screener is used during work breaks for quick checks. Results feed into the Focus List, which is reviewed and updated daily. The Finviz API hack by @erikcarell streamlines the transfer of Finviz screen results directly into TradingView watchlists.
