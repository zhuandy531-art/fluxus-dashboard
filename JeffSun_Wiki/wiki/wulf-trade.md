# Trade Example: WULF

> [!summary]
> WULF (TeraWulf) demonstrated Jeff's process for selecting the single strongest leader within an industry group. From the WGMI (bitcoin mining/crypto mining ETF) group, Jeff picked only WULF -- which then ran +120% in 2 months, emerging as the strongest leader in the sector. The trade illustrates his "Relative Strength first, setup second" philosophy.

## Setup & Entry

Jeff shared his thought process on why he selected WULF specifically, rather than other names in the bitcoin mining group:

> "My thought process on only picking WULF within WGMI group, before it ran +120% in 2 months, emerging as the strongest leader"
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 4]]

The selection process highlights several key principles:

- **Industry group analysis**: Jeff identified the bitcoin mining / energy sector (tracked via WGMI ETF) as showing strength, consistent with his [[top-down-approach]] and [[situational-awareness]] framework.
- **Relative Strength within the group**: Rather than buying the sector ETF or multiple names, Jeff filtered down to the single strongest stock within the group. This follows his hard rule: [[vars-relative-strength|Relative Strength first, setup second]].
- **Screener identification**: The stock likely surfaced through Jeff's [[high-adr-screener]] or strongest-mover screeners, given WULF's characteristics as a high-volatility, thematic small/mid-cap name.

The decision to pick only WULF -- and not spread across multiple mining names -- reflects Jeff's conviction-based approach. He typically carries a concentrated [[focus-list]] rather than diversifying across an entire sector.

## Exit & Result

WULF ran **+120% in 2 months**, confirming Jeff's selection of it as the leader within the WGMI group. The exit timing and method are not detailed in the source material, but Jeff's [[sell-rules]] -- particularly selling into strength using ATR% extension signals -- would have governed the profit-taking.

## Key Lessons

1. **Pick the leader, not the sector**: Jeff does not buy sector baskets. He identifies the single strongest name within a group and concentrates his risk there. WULF validated this approach by outperforming the broader WGMI group.
2. **Relative Strength is the primary filter**: Before evaluating the chart setup, Jeff looks at which stock is showing the most relative strength. WULF stood out from the mining group on this metric.
3. **Thematic awareness without theme-chasing**: Bitcoin mining / energy was a strong theme, but Jeff did not blindly buy the theme. He applied his full screening and focus list process to find the optimal expression of the theme.
4. **Group analysis informs stock selection**: Jeff uses tools like the Industry Group Strength indicator (by @amphtrading) to identify which groups are strong, then drills down to find the leader within that group.
