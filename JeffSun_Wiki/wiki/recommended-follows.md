# Recommended Follows

> [!summary]
> Jeff curates his Twitter feed aggressively, having learned from personal experience that following the wrong accounts delays progress by years. He shares his top 5 Fintwit highlights, chart accounts organized by expertise, and deep spotlights on BrianLeeTrades and LoneStockTrader. He also distills 10 key insights from a Druckenmiller interview.

## Core Concept

> "I Delayed My Own Progress by 3 Years Learning the Wrong Things: Refine Your Twitter Feed By Following These Legitimate Traders Who Share With Real 'Trading Maturity' Without Selling."

Jeff's chapter title says it all. The quality of your information diet directly affects the speed of your development. Following the wrong accounts — those selling courses, hyping trades, or promoting unrealistic returns — actively harms your progress.

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff practices what he preaches — his own Twitter feed is highly curated, and he uses it as a research tool rather than entertainment. His Mindset Principle #4 ([[right-mindset]]) explicitly warns against following others' tips and trades.

The key distinction is between *studying* content (analyzing someone's process, comparing their analysis to yours) and *consuming* content (mindlessly scrolling and copying trades). Jeff recommends the former and warns against the latter.
