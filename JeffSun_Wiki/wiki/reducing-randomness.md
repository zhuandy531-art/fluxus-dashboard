# Reducing Randomness

> [!summary]
> Jeff insists that improvement must begin with reducing randomness in your trading behavior. Consistency in daily habits is the foundation. If your win rate is below 50%, setups are secondary — the real work is in the 3 improvement questions: How can you make more on winners? Lose less on losers? Generate more winning ideas?

## Core Concept

Most struggling traders believe their problem is finding better setups. Jeff disagrees. The primary problem is **randomness** — inconsistent behavior, varying position sizes, haphazard entry timing, and emotional decision-making that makes it impossible to extract signal from noise.

> "And has to begin with reducing Randomness" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1658848875099160583]]

Until randomness is reduced, no amount of setup improvement will help — because you cannot measure what you cannot control.

## Rules / Details

**Begin With Reducing Randomness**
Jeff frames this as the prerequisite for all other improvement. Before optimizing entries, exits, or setups, a trader must first behave consistently enough that their journal data actually means something.

**Consistency in Habits**
Jeff published specific habits that help reduce randomness [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1658968851009409025]]:
- Same screening routine every day
- Same watchlist management process
- Same execution criteria
- Same position sizing method
- Same journaling format

The daily workflow (screening, watchlist management, focus list preparation, situational awareness, stops management) is performed in the same order every day — not because it is the only valid order, but because consistency produces measurable data.

**Stockbee: "Good Traders Are Innovators"**
As Stockbee says: "Good traders are innovators. They find better ways to do things." [[sources/notion/risk-and-position-management-snapshot]]

But innovation comes *after* consistency — you cannot improve what you cannot measure, and you cannot measure what is random.

**Setups Are Secondary If Win Rate Is Below 50%**

> "If You Accepted <50% Win Rate, Setups Are Secondary & Will Never Be Groundbreaking" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1629449806195331072]]

This is a critical insight: if your win rate is below 50% (as Jeff's often is — his lowest monthly win rate was 31.6%), the leverage point is not in finding better entries. It is in:
1. Reducing losses on losers (tighter stops, faster exits)
2. Letting winners run further (better trailing, patience)
3. Better trade selection (fewer but higher-quality ideas)

**The 3 Improvement Questions**
Jeff and the risk management community converge on three questions that drive all meaningful trading improvement:

1. **How can you make more money on your winners?** — Better trailing, wider profit targets, partial-selling discipline
2. **How can you lose less money on your losers?** — Tighter stops, faster recognition of failed trades, the [[3-stop-strategy]]
3. **How can you generate more winning ideas?** — Better screening, sharper focus lists, improved situational awareness

These questions are asked during every [[performance-review]] cycle.

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff's entire system is designed around consistency that reduces randomness:
- The [[3-stop-strategy]] removes discretion from stop-loss execution
- The 15 hard rules in Chapter 6 eliminate common sources of random behavior
- The [[journal-system]] captures data in a standardized format across every trade
- Max 3 new positions per session prevents impulsive over-trading

His advice for traders on the verge of giving up:

> "My Advice If You're On The Verge Of Giving Up Due To Lack Of Profit Momentum, Or Constantly Missing Trades" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1842112913936679299]]

The answer is always the same: reduce randomness first, then let the data guide improvement.
