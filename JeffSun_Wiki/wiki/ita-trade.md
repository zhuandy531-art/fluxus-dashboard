# Trade Example: ITA

> [!summary]
> ITA (iShares U.S. Aerospace & Defense ETF) trade from November 2024 is Jeff's primary example for demonstrating the [[3-stop-strategy]] in practice. As a sector ETF trade, it also illustrates his Druckenmiller-influenced approach of exploring non-correlated asset classes beyond equities for edge.

## Setup & Entry

ITA was identified through Jeff's [[etf-screener|Liquid ETF screener]] (Screener #7), which he uses to find tradable opportunities outside of US-listed individual equities. Jeff frames this approach with a Druckenmiller-inspired philosophy:

> "Exploring ideas in tradable asset classes beyond correlated equities can provide a valuable edge." - Stanley Druckenmiller
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 3]]

The defense sector ETF provided:

- **Non-correlated exposure**: Defense stocks often move independently of the broader tech/growth-driven market, providing portfolio diversification at the trade level.
- **Liquid ETF characteristics**: ITA met Jeff's liquidity requirements for ETF trades, with sufficient average dollar volume to avoid slippage.
- **Sector strength**: The trade aligned with Jeff's [[top-down-approach]] -- identifying sector-level strength before drilling down to the specific instrument.

## Trade Management

Jeff uses the ITA trade as his primary illustration of the [[3-stop-strategy]] mechanics in a real position:

> "An Example with My ITA Trade in November 2024"
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 6]]

The trade was accompanied by a detailed chart showing the 3-stop structure applied to ITA, demonstrating:

1. **Three stop levels at 33% each**: The full position was divided into three equal portions, each with its own stop order.
2. **Capping losses at -0.67R**: Rather than risking -1R on the full position, the layered stops reduced the average loss below -0.67R.
3. **ATR% extension for profit-taking**: As ITA moved in Jeff's favor, [[atr-extension-from-50ma]] signals guided partial profit-taking into strength.

The ITA chart example (visible in Chapter 6 of the Complete Guide) is one of the most referenced visuals in Jeff's educational content for the 3-stop strategy.

## Exit & Result

Specific profit/loss figures for the ITA trade are not documented in the source material. The trade is primarily referenced as a teaching example for the [[3-stop-strategy]] execution, with the detailed chart showing how the three stop levels were placed and managed relative to price action.

## Key Lessons

1. **3-stop strategy in practice**: ITA is the go-to example for understanding how the 3-stop strategy works on a real trade -- not just in theory, but with actual stop levels plotted on a chart.
2. **ETFs as first-class trading instruments**: Jeff does not limit himself to individual equities. His [[etf-screener]] covers liquid ETFs across sectors, commodities, and international markets. ITA shows that the same system works on ETFs.
3. **Druckenmiller's non-correlation principle**: Seeking opportunities outside of correlated equities can provide edge, especially when the broad market is extended or range-bound.
4. **Sector rotation awareness**: Defense as a sector offered relative strength at a time when it was non-correlated to the broader market trend, illustrating the value of [[situational-awareness]] at the sector level.
