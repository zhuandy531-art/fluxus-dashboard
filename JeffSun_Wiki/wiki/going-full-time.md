# Going Full-Time

> [!summary]
> Chapter 10 of Jeff's Complete Guide addresses the hard truths about going full-time: financial prerequisites, why people with good careers should think twice, and which vulnerable groups trading is categorically not suited for. Jeff speaks from experience — he went full-time himself but warns most people against it.

## Core Concept

Going full-time as a trader is one of the most consequential career decisions a person can make. Jeff treats this topic with unusual directness, acknowledging that the romantic appeal of "trading for a living" masks enormous financial and psychological risks. The chapter title itself signals his stance: "Lots of Hard Truth."

## Rules / Details

**Considerations and Financial Needs**

Jeff's primary resource on this topic [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1591446881426898944]] outlines the financial security needed before going full-time:

- Sufficient capital to trade with — not borrowed, not savings you cannot afford to lose
- A financial runway that covers living expenses for an extended period without requiring any trading income
- Proven, documented profitability over multiple market cycles — not just one hot streak
- Understanding that drawdowns will directly impact your ability to pay bills, creating psychological pressure that degrades performance

> "Hard Truth: Trading Part-Time For Income Is A Fallacy" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1647324202519887873]]

Jeff is blunt: part-time trading as an income source is not realistic for most people. The commitment required for proficiency demands full engagement.

**Advice for People With Good Careers**

Jeff explicitly advises caution for those already in strong career positions:

> "My Advice: If You Have Good Career, With Prospect For Growth" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1962911428547096947]]

A good career provides stable income, career growth, benefits, and social structure. Trading eliminates all of these. Jeff suggests that many traders would be better served trading part-time while maintaining their career, rather than making the leap to full-time.

The lower the timeframe, the greater the commitment trading demands [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1848213726815817866]] — swing trading from a daily chart is far more compatible with a career than intraday scalping.

**Vulnerable Groups Trading Isn't Suited For**

Jeff identifies specific groups for whom full-time trading is particularly risky, even with significant capital:

> "My Advice: Vulnerable Groups I Believe Trading Isn't Suited For, Even With Significant Capital" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1949819495482085471]]

This reflects his core belief that trading requires specific psychological traits — emotional resilience, comfort with uncertainty, and the ability to sustain motivation through long losing streaks. Not everyone has these traits, and capital alone cannot substitute for them.

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff himself transitioned to full-time trading and eventually built it into a fund management operation (closed-end vehicle, performance-fee basis, reporting to one party). He also diversified into real estate — 12 properties totaling over $10 million USD by June 2024 [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1803396643687166425]].

This diversification is itself a lesson: even as a successful full-time trader, Jeff did not put all his wealth back into trading. The real estate portfolio provides income stability independent of market conditions.

His closing remarks reference his own early mindset being "couldn't have been more wrong" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1749679698475626560]], suggesting that the decision to go full-time should be made with realistic expectations, not youthful overconfidence.

> "Longevity To A Trading Career Requires Solid Daily Routine. This Is Non-Negotiable" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1499677167193837568]]
