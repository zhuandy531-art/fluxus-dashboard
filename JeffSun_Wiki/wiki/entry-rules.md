# 15 Hard Rules for Trade Entry

> [!summary]
> Jeff Sun's 15 non-negotiable execution rules govern every trade entry. They filter out suboptimal setups at the moment of execution, ensuring that even an A-rated idea only becomes a live position when real-time conditions confirm the opportunity.

## Core Concept

> "An astounding trading idea, when it comes up for execution in the live market, must be reassessed by its risk-to-reward potential based on the price action at that *moment*."

These rules sit between the Focus List (pre-market preparation) and actual order execution. A stock can pass every screening criterion and still be rejected at the point of entry if any hard rule is violated.

## The 15 Hard Rules

### Risk-Based Filters

1. **No entry if LoD exceeds 60% ATR.** If the low of the day has already consumed more than 60% of the stock's average true range, the remaining upside within the day's volatility envelope is too compressed. See [[lod-atr-rule]] for the full calculation.

2. **No entry if ATR% from 50-MA exceeds 4x multiples.** A stock extended more than 4 ATR% multiples from its 50-day moving average has already launched -- the launchpad is behind it. See [[execution-quality]] for the launchpad metaphor.

3. **No biotech.** Biotechs carry binary gap risk from FDA decisions and trial data. For biotech exposure, Jeff uses only the LABD or LABU inverse/leveraged ETFs via IBB/XBI.

4. **No entry without substantial RVOL.** Outside of mega cap liquid names and liquid ETFs, price always fades without relative volume confirmation. See [[rvol-based-entry]].

### Timing Filters

5. **Delay entry 30 minutes post-open, unless extreme high RVOL surfaces.** The first 30 minutes are noise-dominated. Only exceptional volume run-rates justify early action.

6. **No entry before major economic data or earnings.** No new positions prior to immediate pre-market major economic data releases, or pre/post market earnings announcements.

### Trend & Structure Filters

7. **No entry against a declining 200-MA.** > "I will not enter a trade against its declining 200-MA because this always happens" -- the stock reverts to the declining trend.

8. **No entry into an immediate gap resistance zone.** Overhead supply from unfilled gaps creates selling pressure that undermines breakout entries.

### Position Management Filters

9. **Max 3 new positions per session.** Jeff always rolls risk as the market confirms entry momentum from the prior day, rather than front-loading exposure.

10. **Never chase -- only the most optimal entry.** > "Missing trades won't make you go broke, chasing them often will." Even being one day late disqualifies entry if the optimal level has passed.

11. **More caution on consecutive up days.** The more the market rises over consecutive sessions, the more cautious Jeff becomes about initiating new long positions.

12. **After T+3 portfolio established, prioritize adding existing positions.** Once a durable portfolio is beyond T+3, those positions are already above breakeven stops and have proven leadership. Priority shifts to adding size on winners rather than opening new names.

13. **Extreme caution layering risk on gap-up opens.** Adding further exposure when the market is poised to gap up invites immediate drawdown if the gap fades.

### Structural Rules

14. **Always "long" -- use inverse ETFs for shorts.** Even short ideas based on weakness are expressed through available inverse long synthetic ETFs (e.g., LABD, AMZD, SQQQ).

15. **Rule #15 is subscriber-only.** > "Since I prefer to keep this from being widely adopted, this strict execution rule #15 is shared exclusively with my Twitter/X subscribers and described in my daily Focus List."

## How Jeff Applies It

These rules function as a pre-flight checklist at the moment of execution. After a price alert fires on a Focus List name, Jeff evaluates every hard rule before placing an order. The rules are designed to be binary -- pass/fail with no gray area -- which eliminates discretionary second-guessing during live market hours.

The rules compound with each other: a stock might clear the LoD/ATR filter but fail the RVOL check, or pass both but violate the gap resistance rule. All 14 accessible rules must be satisfied simultaneously.
