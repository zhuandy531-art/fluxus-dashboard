# RVOL (Relative Volume)

> [!summary]
> RVOL measures current volume relative to the average (typically 50-day sessions). Jeff treats RVOL as a non-negotiable entry filter -- strong relative volume is the first proof of institutional buying, and its absence is reason enough to pass on a trade. He credits RVOL-based rules with saving him from countless unnecessary stop losses year after year.

## Core Concept

**RVOL** = Current Volume / Average Volume (50-day)

Expressed as a percentage, RVOL tells you whether today's trading activity is above or below normal. An RVOL of 200% means the stock is trading at twice its average daily volume at the current point in the session.

RVOL is displayed in Jeff's **Swing Data** indicator ([TradingView script](https://www.tradingview.com/v/uloAa2EI/)), which also shows Projected Volume -- the estimated total volume for the day based on the current run rate.

## Entry Confirmation

> Using RVOL for entry confirmation: Strong volume run-rate as first proof of strong buying for exceptional short term return.

Jeff's +180% KC trade over 6 weeks began with an RVOL-confirmed entry. The strong volume run-rate at the point of breakout was the first signal that institutional buyers were participating, validating the trade before price action alone could confirm it.

**Hard rule**: Jeff does not enter trades outside of mega-cap liquid watchlist and liquid ETFs if there is no substantial RVOL -- because price always fades outside of RVOL.

## RVOL on ORH (Opening Range High)

> RVOL based entry on ORH have proven statistical edge in the market.

Combining RVOL with the Opening Range High (ORH) reclaim creates a statistically validated entry signal. Jeff runs 2 live market screeners on TradingView specifically to monitor high RVOL names in real time during the session.

## RVOL Saving from Unnecessary Stops

> RVOL based entry rules have saved me from countless unnecessary stop losses year after year.

By requiring RVOL confirmation before entering, Jeff avoids trades where price may technically break out but lacks the volume conviction to sustain the move. Without volume, breakouts are more likely to fail and trigger stops.

## Defining High RVOL: Projected Volume with 50% Haircut

> If you find it hard to define what qualifies as high RVOL, use Projected Volume in the script with a 50% haircut.

For traders unsure what threshold constitutes "high" RVOL, Jeff recommends using the **Projected Volume** feature in his Swing Data indicator and applying a **50% haircut** (discount) to the projection. If the discounted projected volume still exceeds the 50-day average, that qualifies as genuinely high RVOL. This conservative approach filters out false signals from early-session volume spikes that fade by close.

## How Jeff Applies It

- **Hard entry rule**: No entry without substantial RVOL (outside mega-cap/liquid ETF watchlist)
- **Live monitoring**: 2 TradingView screeners dedicated to tracking RVOL in real time
- **M30 Re-ORH**: Delays entry 30 minutes post-open unless extreme high RVOL surfaces
- **Loss prevention**: RVOL filter eliminates trades that would otherwise result in failed breakouts and unnecessary stops
- **Projected volume**: Uses projected volume with 50% haircut as the practical threshold for defining high RVOL
