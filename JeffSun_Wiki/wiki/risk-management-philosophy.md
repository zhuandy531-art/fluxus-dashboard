# Risk Management Philosophy

> [!summary]
> Jeff's core philosophy is that the "best loser is the long-term winner." Trading is a big data game governed by math, not opinions. The trader's job is to control absolute dollar risk on every trade, accept unrealized profit volatility, and let the asymmetry of one outsized winner covering many losers compound over time.

## Core Concept

Jeff treats trading as a **probability-driven data game** where the individual outcome of any single trade is irrelevant. What matters is the statistical edge expressed across hundreds or thousands of trades.

> "Profitable trading is not about opinions, or single great stock pick. It is about math." [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1456088827894718470]]

> "The best loser is the long-term winner." — Phantom of the Pits, a quote Jeff references repeatedly [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1750532256446496997]]

The philosophy rests on accepting that you will be wrong most of the time. Jeff's max monthly win rate in 2024 was only 31.6%. The edge comes not from being right more often, but from **controlling losses** and **letting winners run** to asymmetric multiples.

## Rules / Details

**Trading as a Big Data Game**
- Every trade is one data point in a long series — think in hundreds of trades, not individual outcomes [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1658298850870964224]]
- The greatest improvements in trading performance come from analyzing yourself, not finding better setups [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1800408834231713859]]
- A trading strategy may offer positive expected value over time, but the trading edge does not stem from the strategy alone [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1783396490985001118]]

**Controlling Absolute Dollar Risk**
- You cannot control the outcome, but you can always control your absolute dollar risk in every trade [[sources/notion/right-mindset-snapshot]]
- Enter as close as possible to where you are wrong on the trade — tight entries have a parabolic effect on R-returns [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1948260986143801469]]
- The [[3-stop-strategy]] caps average losses to -0.6 to -0.8R instead of -1R

**Unrealized Profit Volatility**
Jeff describes the psychological challenge of managing large unrealized gains:

> "As your equity grows (especially when it surpasses your absolute dollar risk tolerance relative to your monthly expenses), seeing unrealized profit drop from, say, +$500,000 to +$280,000 in a single session can be hard to handle, even if you're following textbook rules." [[sources/notion/risk-and-position-management-snapshot]]

> "I believe not many can emotionally withstand holding 8-10 full-sized trades with 80% portfolio utilization, especially when trading with a bankroll that significantly impacts their quality of life and that of their dependents."

This is why Jeff sells partials into strength — to lock in realized profit and reduce unrealized profit volatility.

## How Jeff Applies It

**One Trade to Cover Many Losses**

> "One trade to more than cover 13 losing streaks is my style." [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1749460835662225835]]

In 2024, Jeff had winning trades as high as 51R, 4 trades at 20R+, and 11 at 10R+. These outlier winners — often the ones you least expect to make a huge move — are what drive annual performance.

> "Who'd have thought $KC could make a 200% gain in a month? Learn to hold your position and ride winners. The outlier positions that can make an impact on your % performance are often those that you do not expect to make a huge move."

**Math, Not Opinions**

Jeff frames 6% monthly compounding as yielding 100%+ annualized returns [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1795402114891690414]], and demonstrates that even a 27% win rate can produce 114% annual gains with proper risk control [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1940818944228839430]]. The numbers work when you trust the math over your feelings.
