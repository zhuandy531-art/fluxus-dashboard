# Limiting New Positions

> [!summary]
> Jeff enforces a hard rule of maximum 3 new positions per session and strongly urges traders to track their "hyper-active" days in their journal. He uses the SPY 4x ATR% from 50-MA as a caution signal for new long exposure and sees pullbacks as opportunity rather than threat.

## Core Concept

One of the most impactful risk management rules is simply *limiting how many new positions you take*. Jeff considers this an immediately actionable fix — unlike improving setups or strategy, which takes months, capping trade frequency can be implemented today.

> "Another rule that I strongly urge everyone to explore and implement is reducing and setting a limit to your daily and/or weekly new position frequency." [[sources/notion/risk-and-position-management-snapshot]]

## Rules / Details

**Hard Rule: Max 3 New Positions Per Session**
- Jeff does not execute more than 3 new positions per session (Chapter 6, Rule #9)
- He always rolls risk forward as the market confirms entry momentum from prior days
- Once a durable portfolio is established (beyond T+3), priority shifts to adding onto existing proven positions rather than opening new ones [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1968946399740940605]]

**Track Hyper-Active Days in Your Journal**

> "Go to your journal and track that 1-3 days duration (MoM) that you are 'hyper' actively executing new position — is the outcome desirable? If not, taking on too many new positions will always be a trading pitfall, and you can fix this immediately." [[sources/notion/risk-and-position-management-snapshot]]

This is a data-driven exercise: pull up your journal, find the days where you opened the most positions, and check the aggregate outcome. Most traders discover those are their worst days.

**SPY 4x ATR% from 50-MA Caution Signal**

> "I will be reluctant to new risk exposure when $SPY is 4x ATR% from 50-MA when it historically pulls back from 5x ATR% from 50-MA." [[sources/notion/risk-and-position-management-snapshot]]

Jeff also applies this rule in the opposite direction — the more the market rises over consecutive days, the more cautious he becomes about new long positions (Chapter 6, Rule #11). He is extremely cautious with layering further risk exposure when the market is poised to open with a gap up [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1968650833211347437]].

**Pullbacks Create Opportunity**
Christian Fromhertz articulates the counterintuitive benefit of pullbacks:

> "If you've been doing this for a while, you actually like these little bit of pullbacks. You don't want to be buying things when the sentiment is super, super hot in momentum areas and in growth areas. You want things to come in a little bit, right? Because it resets things." [[sources/notion/risk-and-position-management-snapshot]]

This relates to market cycles — the later you are within a swing cycle, the more prone to failure breakouts become, which is why Qullamaggie says things like "we just need a quick flush."

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff's approach combines frequency discipline with situational awareness. On days where the market is extended (SPY 4x+ ATR% from 50-MA), he may take zero new positions even if setups appear valid. When the market pulls back and resets, he increases activity — but still never exceeds 3 new entries per session.

His daily focus list, shared with Twitter/X subscribers, reflects this discipline: some days feature 5-8 focus names, but the execution plan always caps at 3 new entries maximum.

> "I strongly believe in the edge of knowing when to go 'heavy' vs 'light' in terms of trade frequency and activity." [[sources/notion/risk-and-position-management-snapshot]]
