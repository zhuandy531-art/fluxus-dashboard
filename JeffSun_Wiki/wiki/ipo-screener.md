# IPO and High Short Float Screeners

> [!summary]
> Jeff runs two specialized weekly screeners exclusively on Finviz: the IPO screener and the High Short Float screener. Both rely on Finviz-only filtering parameters that are not available on TradingView, and they operate on a weekly rather than daily cadence.

## Core Concept

These two screeners target distinct market niches that frequently produce outsized moves:

- **IPOs** -- Recently listed companies with limited price history but potentially explosive supply/demand dynamics. New issues that base and break out can become the strongest leaders of a cycle.
- **High Short Float** -- Stocks with elevated short interest as a percentage of float. When combined with other catalysts (earnings, volume surges, technical breakouts), high short float creates conditions for short squeezes and accelerated price moves.

Both characteristics align with Jeff's observation that the best percentage performers tend to have low market cap, low float percentage, and high short float.

> "The best % performers almost always have these three ingredients."

## Rules / Details

### IPO Screener (Weekly)

**Platform**: Finviz only -- Finviz's filtering parameters are uniquely capable of screening for IPO-specific criteria such as IPO date ranges and post-IPO price behavior.

**Frequency**: Weekly. Unlike the daily post-market screeners, IPOs are screened on a weekly basis since the pace of new listings does not require daily monitoring.

The screener identifies recently listed stocks that are beginning to form constructive bases after their initial trading period. Jeff looks for IPOs that show signs of institutional accumulation and volatility contraction.

### High Short Float Screener (Weekly)

**Platform**: Finviz only -- Finviz provides short float percentage as a native filtering parameter, which TradingView's screener does not offer.

**Frequency**: Weekly. Short interest data updates on a bi-monthly basis (twice per month via exchange reports), so weekly screening captures changes without redundant daily runs.

The screener filters for stocks where short interest represents a significant percentage of the float, flagging potential short squeeze candidates. These names are cross-referenced with Jeff's other screeners -- a stock appearing on both the High Short Float screen and the High ADR% screen, for example, warrants closer attention.

## How Jeff Applies It

Both screeners feed into the broader watchlist management process. Because they run weekly rather than daily, they serve as a supplementary layer that surfaces names the daily screeners might miss. Jeff reviews these weekly results alongside his daily screener output when updating watchlists.

IPO candidates that pass further focus list criteria (relative strength, VCP, volume confirmation) may be promoted to the Focus List. High short float names are treated with awareness of the additional volatility risk -- the same short squeeze dynamics that create opportunity also create wider intraday ranges and gap risk.
