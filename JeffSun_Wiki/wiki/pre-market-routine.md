# Pre-Market Routine

> [!summary]
> Jeff Sun's pre-market routine bridges the gap between post-market Focus List preparation and live market execution. The 2023 version centers on four tools used 30 minutes before the open. The routine is designed to be time-efficient and sustainable -- because the lower the timeframe, the greater the commitment it demands. A daily fitness routine before the market opens is strongly recommended for mental clarity.

## Core Concept

> "The importance of a daily routine lies in being time-efficient, manageable, and sustainable for long-term trading longevity -- it cannot be exhausting."

> "The lower the timeframe, the greater the commitment it demands from you."

The pre-market routine is not about cramming in more analysis. The heavy lifting was done post-market the night before (screening, watchlist management, Focus List preparation, situational awareness). The morning session is about transitioning from preparation to readiness: confirming market context, setting price alerts, and getting mentally prepared for execution.

## The 2023 Routine

Jeff shared a brief overview of his routine in 2023, then elaborated with a detailed breakdown of the four tools he uses 30 minutes before the open. These tools provide the final pre-market check on:

1. **Market context** -- Overnight futures action, pre-market gappers, and key economic data releases
2. **Focus List status** -- Which names from the Focus List are showing pre-market activity or gap behavior
3. **Price alerts** -- Setting alerts on Focus List names at key levels (see [[price-alerts]])
4. **RVOL monitoring** -- TradingView pre-market gapper screener and Focus List-based screener for identifying names with elevated relative volume before the bell

Jeff also runs 2 live market screeners on TradingView: one based on the Focus List and one based on pre-market gappers. His mainframe setup for live market trading includes RVOL monitoring prominently displayed.

## Six Earnings Price Action Outcomes

For traders who trade or chase earnings, Jeff outlines **six outcomes of end-of-day price action post-earnings** that must be understood:

1. Gap up, hold/extend
2. Gap up, fade
3. Gap down, hold/continue lower
4. Gap down, recover
5. Flat open, trend direction emerges
6. Flat open, chop/indecision

Understanding which category the post-earnings price action falls into determines whether a PEAD (Post-Earnings Announcement Drift) opportunity exists or whether the move is a trap. Jeff emphasizes studying this framework before trading around earnings events.

## Timeframe and Commitment

Jeff makes an explicit point about the relationship between timeframe and commitment. As a swing trader operating on daily/weekly charts, his required live-market commitment is manageable. Day traders and scalpers face exponentially greater demands on their time and attention. The pre-market routine is calibrated for swing trading sustainability.

## Fitness for Mental Clarity

> "I strongly recommend maintaining a daily fitness routine for mental clarity. Personally, I do mine before the market opens -- it makes a real difference."

Jeff's fitness routine is not an afterthought -- it is a deliberate component of his pre-market preparation. Physical activity before the session clears mental fog and builds the discipline that carries into trading decisions. This parallels his broader philosophy that consistency in process (not talent) drives results.

## How Jeff Applies It

The pre-market window is approximately 30 minutes. Jeff arrives at his workstation with the post-market preparation already complete. The morning is about confirming conditions, not discovering new ideas. Price alerts are set on Focus List names at breakout levels, RVOL is monitored for confirmation signals, and situational awareness from the prior evening is updated with overnight developments.

Jeff's hard rule of delaying entry by 30 minutes post-open (unless extreme RVOL surfaces) means the pre-market routine flows directly into a disciplined waiting period at the open, reinforcing patience.
