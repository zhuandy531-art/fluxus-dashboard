# Trade Example: RGTI

> [!summary]
> RGTI (Rigetti Computing) is cited by Jeff as a prime example of tight execution from the focus list. On 9th September 2025, the trade demonstrated how disciplined entry at the optimal level -- with an extremely tight stop -- can produce outsized R-multiples (+40-80R). Jeff references Qullamaggie's execution of this same idea to illustrate the power of trading tight.

## Setup & Entry

RGTI appeared on Jeff's [[focus-list]] as a quantum computing momentum play. The key detail Jeff highlights is how *tight* the entry was executed:

> "This is how tight Quallamaggie traded RGTI on 9th September 2025 from my focus list"
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 4]]

The trade exemplifies Jeff's core [[entry-rules]]:

- **[[lod-atr-rule]]**: The entry was executed at the breakout level without the Low of Day exceeding 60% ATR -- Jeff's hard rule for qualifying entries.
- **RVOL confirmation**: Consistent with [[rvol-based-entry]], the trade required strong relative volume to confirm institutional buying.
- **Focus list graduation**: RGTI had been tracked on the watchlist, showed relative strength and a volatility contraction pattern (VCP), and was upgraded to the focus list before the breakout day.

The quantum computing theme provided the narrative catalyst, but Jeff's process is setup-agnostic -- the chart structure and [[execution-quality]] determined whether the idea was actionable.

## Trade Management

Jeff documents this as one of the most extreme R-multiple outcomes in his shared examples:

> "This is how Quallamaggie's RGTI trade on 9th September 2025 gain beyond +40-80R"
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 6]]

The extraordinary R-multiple was possible because:

1. **Extremely tight initial risk**: The tighter the entry (less distance between entry and stop), the smaller the R-unit, and therefore the larger the R-multiple for any given price move.
2. **[[t-plus-3-framework]]**: After surviving the T+3 window, the position was held with [[trailing-stops]] that allowed the trend to develop without premature exit.
3. **Patience with time**: Jeff's principle of letting the x-axis (time) work -- "Don't get fixated on price movements; be patient with the factor of time" -- allowed the full move to unfold.

## Exit & Result

The specific exit details are not fully documented in the source material, but the trade achieved **+40-80R** -- meaning the profit was 40 to 80 times the initial risk amount. This is consistent with Jeff's observation that:

> "One trade to more than cover 13 losing streaks is my style"
> — [[sources/notion/complete-guide-snapshot|Complete Guide, Chapter 6]]

A +40R trade at 1% risk per trade would represent a +40% portfolio gain from a single position.

## Key Lessons

1. **Tight execution has a parabolic effect on R-returns**: Jeff cites research showing that executing trades at much tighter risk has a parabolic (not linear) effect on R-multiples. RGTI is the clearest demonstration of this principle.
2. **Focus list quality matters**: The trade came directly from Jeff's focus list -- not from chasing a gapper or reacting to news. The preparation was done post-market, and the entry was executed when conditions were met.
3. **Theme provides fuel, execution provides edge**: Quantum computing was the theme, but the edge came from the tight entry, not the narrative.
4. **Cross-reference with peers**: Jeff notes that Qullamaggie also traded RGTI from the same focus list, reinforcing that the process identifies the same high-conviction setups across experienced traders.
