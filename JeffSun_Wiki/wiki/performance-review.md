# Performance Review

> [!summary]
> Jeff considers a periodic review process non-negotiable for remaining profitable. Key review insights include reducing average holding period for losses, understanding how 6% monthly compounding yields 100%+ annual returns, and how even a 27% win rate can produce 114% annualized gains. He urges keeping journals from every period — especially the bad ones.

## Core Concept

The performance review is not optional. It is the mechanism by which a trader converts raw journal data into actionable improvements. Without it, the journal is just a log file nobody reads.

> "A Period Review Process is Non-Negotiable to Remain Profitable" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1670213508095692800]]

Jeff treats the review as an investigation — looking for patterns, anomalies, and drift in his own behavior relative to his rules.

## Rules / Details

**Reducing Average Holding Period for Losses**
One of the most actionable findings from Jeff's own reviews: holding losing trades too long destroys returns. By tracking the average number of days a losing trade is held, traders can identify and fix this pattern.

> "Reducing Average Holding Period For Losses: Food For Thought On Position Holding of Unrealized Losses" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1805502341946523702]]

The [[3-stop-strategy]] directly addresses this — the layered stop system forces partial exits early, mechanically reducing the average hold time for losers.

**The Compounding Math**
Jeff breaks down what realistic performance looks like when reviewed over proper timeframes:

- **6% monthly compound = 100%+ annualized** [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1795402114891690414]] — A breakdown of what it takes to achieve this seemingly modest monthly target, which compounds to life-changing annual performance
- **27% win rate = 114% annualized** [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1940818944228839430]] — A more conservative approach demonstrating that win rate is almost irrelevant when R-multiples are managed properly

These numbers only become visible through rigorous period review — day-to-day, the equity curve feels chaotic.

**Keep Your Journals From Bad Periods**
Jeff explicitly warns against deleting or ignoring journal data from losing periods:

> "No matter how poorly you think you've traded in the past, keep your trade journals. Don't shy away from the reality of your past performance. Instead, review them and take pride in how much you've grown through your journey." [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1805086493897289860]]

Bad periods contain the most valuable data — they reveal which rules were broken, which market conditions exposed weaknesses, and where the biggest improvements can be made.

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff's review process examines:
- **Win rate by month** — to identify seasonal patterns and market regime sensitivity
- **Average R per winning trade vs. average R per losing trade** — the core ratio that determines profitability
- **Holding period distribution** — separate for winners and losers
- **Hyper-active days** — when too many new positions were opened (see [[limiting-new-positions]])
- **P&L histogram shape** — from the [[journal-system]] Part 5

He annotates every trade — win or loss — on the index chart to evaluate situational awareness [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1922500217607487503]]. This visual overlay reveals whether he was trading in alignment with or against the market trend.

The review is what transforms a 27% win rate from a psychologically devastating number into a proven path to 114% annual returns — because the review shows the math is working.
