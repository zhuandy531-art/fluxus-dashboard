# Watchlist Management

> [!summary]
> Screening only builds a generic watchlist -- actionable trade ideas come exclusively from active watchlist management. Jeff segments his watchlist into three rotating sets reviewed on a day-by-day basis and maintains a separate "Back Watchlist" for calibrating screeners. During volatile markets, watchlist activity intensifies dramatically, as demonstrated by the rapid Focus List evolution during Aug 22-26, 2025.

## Core Concept

> "Screening only builds a generic watchlist. Actionable ideas only come after watchlist management."

This is a commonly misunderstood skill for new traders. Many believe that running screeners is the end of the process, but Jeff emphasizes that screening is merely the first step. The real work -- and the real edge -- lives in how you manage, curate, and evolve the watchlist over time.

## Rules / Details

### Screening Produces Raw Material

Jeff runs 14 post-market screeners covering CANSLIM-inspired setups, high ADR% movers, extended bases, strongest movers across multiple timeframes (1-week, 1-month, 3-month, 6-month), IPOs, high short float names, liquid ETFs, and screen-within-screen watchlist filters. Results are shifted into dedicated watchlists organized by purpose.

### Three Rotating Sets

If maintaining a consistent watchlist feels challenging, Jeff recommends **segmenting it into three sets and reviewing them on a rotating, day-by-day basis**. This prevents the watchlist from becoming overwhelming and ensures every name gets regular attention without requiring exhaustive daily reviews of the entire universe.

### The Back Watchlist

Beyond the active watchlist, Jeff maintains an additional **"Back Watchlist"** for ideas that serve a different purpose: **calibrating screeners**. These are names that did not make the primary watchlist cut but provide feedback on whether the screener parameters are catching the right types of stocks. If a stock that should have been flagged was missed, the screener settings need adjustment.

### Mega Cap Liquid Watchlist

Jeff also maintains a fixed watchlist of **liquid mega cap names** (exceeding $1B average dollar volume on a constant basis), along with their synthetic leveraged ETFs for higher ADR% opportunities. He identifies opportunities within this list using a distinct process, including shorting via inverse ETFs (e.g., AMZN short via AMZD long).

## How Jeff Applies It

### Volatile Market Periods Demand Intensity

When market volatility picks up, Jeff stays **highly active** in managing the watchlist. The Aug 22-26, 2025 sequence illustrates how fast the Focus List evolves:

- **Aug 22**: Educational thread on Focus List construction during volatile conditions
- **Aug 23**: Focus List and portfolio update showcasing rapid evolution
- **Aug 26**: Another update -- Jeff notes this can be the most lucrative opportunity of the year, requiring active screening

The message is clear: high-volatility environments are not days to sit in cash. They demand aggressive watchlist management because the strongest leaders emerge quickly and the window to identify them is narrow.

### Ongoing Portfolio Context

By September 2025, Jeff's guidance shifted: if you have already built a durable portfolio, priority should be lurking for opportunities to add to existing positions while being highly selective with new exposure. By October 2025, he counseled against second-guessing partial profit-taking or eventual exits -- a 10-MA sell rule can keep you in a position for as long as 5 months.

### Practical Workflow

Jeff consolidated a 3-minute video demonstrating how he screens all 14 screeners and transfers results into various watchlists daily. The process is systematic: screener results flow into purpose-specific watchlists, which are then reviewed and curated into the Focus List.
