# Drawdown Management

> [!summary]
> Jeff suffered 3 successive months of drawdown in 2022 and shares hard-won lessons on the difference between *reducing* drawdown and merely *pausing* it. He advocates knowing what "normal" looks like for your system, using progressive exposure, and facing drawdowns directly rather than escaping them.

## Core Concept

Drawdowns are an inevitable feature of any trading system. The key is not to avoid them entirely, but to understand what your system's drawdown profile looks like and manage through it with discipline. Jeff distinguishes between two fundamentally different responses:

1. **Reducing drawdown** — actively improving your process, tightening execution, and managing risk better
2. **Pausing drawdown** — simply stepping away, which only freezes your equity curve without solving anything

> "Simply reducing trade frequency or stepping away from trading doesn't truly manage drawdown — it just pauses activity in your equity curve. Avoiding action is an escape from reality, and to improve, it's essential to face issues directly and resolve them." [[sources/notion/risk-and-position-management-snapshot]]

## Rules / Details

**Jeff's 2022 Drawdown Experience**
Jeff openly shared that he suffered 3 successive months of drawdown in 2022 — a period that tested his process and psychology [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1560650397043539968]]. He kept trading, kept journaling, and used the data to improve.

**Know What "Normal" Looks Like**
Marios Stamatoudis emphasizes knowing the reality of your strategy — understanding typical drawdown depth and duration so you don't panic during expected variance [[sources/notion/risk-and-position-management-snapshot]].

Jeff reinforces this: if you understand the "normal" behavior of stocks you specialize in, you can adopt sell rules that align to that behavior [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1867218691873804465]].

**Michael Nauss CMT Drawdown Visualization**
One of Jeff's 6 key internalization graphics (Chapter 9) is Michael Nauss CMT's drawdown visualization — a tool that helps traders deeply understand what drawdown on their system will actually look like [[sources/tweets/MichaelNaussCMT-1844133610003316813]]. Knowing the shape and depth of expected drawdowns prevents emotional overreaction.

**Minervini Progressive Exposure**
During recovering markets or uncertain conditions, Jeff applies the concept of progressive exposure — gradually increasing position size and frequency as the market confirms direction, rather than going heavy immediately after a drawdown period.

**Boring Markets**
When the market offers no actionable setups, Jeff references the Japanese proverb:

> "When Fishermen Cannot Go To The Sea, They Repair Nets" [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1894192503219523789]]

Boring markets are for refining process, reviewing journals, and calibrating screeners — not for forcing trades.

## How Jeff Applies It

Jeff's [[3-stop-strategy]] is specifically designed to create a *gradual* decline of the equity curve during losing streaks, versus the *parabolic* growth during winning streaks. By capping losses at -0.6 to -0.8R instead of -1R per trade, the impact of 10 consecutive losers is dramatically reduced [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1647931380033257475]].

His fixed % risk to equity also provides automatic drawdown protection — as equity shrinks, absolute dollar risk shrinks proportionally, preventing the hole from getting deeper at the same rate.

> "No matter how poorly you think you've traded in the past, keep your trade journals. Don't shy away from the reality of your past performance. Instead, review them and take pride in how much you've grown through your journey." [[sources/tweets/jfsrev-1805086493897289860]]
