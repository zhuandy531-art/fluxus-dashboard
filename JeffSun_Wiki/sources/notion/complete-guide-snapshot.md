## **Chapter 1. Glossary of Terms and Expressions I Often Use in my **[**Twitter Commentary**](https://x.com/jfsrev/creator-subscriptions/subscribe)
- **LoD**: Low of intraday price action *at the time*
- **RVOL**: Relative volume compared to the average (typically 50-Day sessions)
- **T+3**: T = execution day; +1 = Day 1 after execution; +3 = Day 3 after execution. ‘Day' does not include weekends or market holiday.
- [**VARS**](https://x.com/jfsrev/status/1909486454260351162)[: Volatility Adjusted Relative Strength](https://x.com/jfsrev/status/1909486454260351162)
- **VARW**: Volatility Adjusted Relative Weakness
- **ORH**: Intraday opening range high *at the time*
- **R**: “I closed the trade on +16R” etc. The “R” in R-Multiple represents the **initial risk** on a trade, defined by the difference between the entry price and the stop-loss level. An R-Multiple then expresses the profit or loss of a trade as a multiple of this initial “R” amount, allowing traders to objectively evaluate performance by focusing on the quality of trades relative to the risk taken
- **M30 Re-ORH**: Post-open 30 minutes reclaim of the intraday opening range high
- [**X × ATR% from 50-MA**](https://x.com/jfsrev/status/1671541248044457986)[: X ATR% multiple extension from the 50-day moving average.](https://x.com/jfsrev/status/1671541248044457986)
- [**ATR:**](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000734653-how-are-adr-and-atr-calculated/)[ Average True Range, & ](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000734653-how-are-adr-and-atr-calculated/)[**ADR:**](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000734653-how-are-adr-and-atr-calculated/)[ Average Daily Range, and their differences](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000734653-how-are-adr-and-atr-calculated/)
- [**VCP**](https://x.com/jfsrev/status/1806188623735509403)[: Volatility Contraction Pattern, popularized by Mark Minervini](https://x.com/jfsrev/status/1806188623735509403). To upgrade a watchlist stock into actionable focus list will require some form of VCP in price, or both price & volume. Every sustainable price expansion rally will always be preceded by a phase of price contraction/tightening. I will NEVER enter a stock with prior loose price action.
- [**PEAD: Post-Earnings Announcement Drift**](https://x.com/jfsrev/status/1838201736043057192)**: **is a well-documented market anomaly where stock prices continue to drift in the direction of an earnings surprise for some time following the announcement. This provides traders with an opportunity to exploit the delayed response of the market to new earnings information.

### **Key Annual Market Dates to Know**
- **Options Expiration:**
	Occurs on the **third Friday of every month** (for standard monthly options).
- **Rebalancing Activities:**
	1. **Quarter-End Fund & ETF Rebalancing:** Typically occurs around **the last few days of each quarter** (March, June, September, December).
	2. **Annual Fund & ETF Rebalancing:** Often done **at year-end (around December 31)**.
	3. **Index Reconstitutions:**
		- **S&P 500 (SPX):** Adjustments generally align with **the third Friday of each quarter**, coinciding with index futures expiration (“triple witching”).
		- **Russell 2000 (IWM):** Major reconstitution takes place **on the fourth Friday of June** each year.
	4. **MSCI Index Rebalancing:** Happens **semiannually**, in **May and November**.
	5. **FTSE Index Rebalancing:** Occurs **quarterly** — in **March, June, September, and December**.
