## Tweet Content

Got asked about my move from UK to US markets.

I’m in the UK, so UK stocks seemed the obvious starting point. But I quickly realised that liquidity was a massive issue, especially in growth stocks, and that the key traders I was learning from all traded US stocks.

So, I made the switch.

Stock selection and liquidity instantly became better, but I had to switch brokers (from a UK to a US one) to get full access to stocks and not pay high fees.

Time zones was the key thing to get used to. Struggled with alertness at market close, and afterwards, when prepping the next day. Particularly as I had a day job, so couldn’t just get up later.

In the end, it was a matter of lifestyle changes:

• Avoid caffeine first thing in the morning.
• Avoid carbs and processed sugar, eat more nuts and berries.
• Exercise and piano playing at lunchtime (before market open, after my focused writing in the morning) to clear my mind. Psychologically, I feel like I’m restarting my day that way.

Some of these were changes I made to become less light sensitive… only to realise that higher light tolerance wasn’t the only benefit. Improved cognitive function and the ability to focus for much longer were massive ‘side effects’.

I was still quite new to trading when I made the switch, so had lots to learn about chart nuances, position sizing, themes and situational awareness, and more.

But chart patterns and fundamental principles aren’t unique to US (or UK) stocks, so I didn’t have to make significant strategy changes due to the switch. The changes to my ‘actual’ trading were just part of my growth as a swing/stock trader generally, and not specific to the market I was trading.

Hope that helps!
