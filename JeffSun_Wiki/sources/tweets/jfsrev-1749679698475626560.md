## Tweet Content

This was my tweet 11 years ago.

I used to exhibit behavior that involved getting excited about short term unrealized profits and believing I had cracked the secret code to the market. It took me a further few years to recognize the valuable learning lesson on the importance of tempered expectations, the need for a realistic understanding of long term game in the market to play out your mathematical edge, and the importance of experience with skin in the game over time. 

Achieving profitability in a single week doesn't guarantee success in the following week, just as being profitable within a month doesn't ensure continued success in the subsequent month. Similarly, enjoying profitability over the course of a year doesn't automatically translate to sustained success this year. This is why I am reliant on the mathematical data of my own trading, much more than predicting what the market could do tomorrow.

I hope this retrospective insight would help guide your current approach with a more grounded perspective, emphasizing the continuous learning journey in the financial landscape.
