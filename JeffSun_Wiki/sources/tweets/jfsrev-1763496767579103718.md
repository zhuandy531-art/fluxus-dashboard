## Tweet Content

I do not use a fixed % extension from 50-MA on individual stocks because you need to account for the volatility adjustment of the stock price daily average range behaviour. 

When $TSLA was a stock in play back in 2019-2021, its volatility was as high as 6.5% ATR. If you fixate a % from 50-MA to take partial profit, you will be missing out a big chunk of the 3-4 rally leg it did. Today, $TSLA is neglected at 3.8% ATR, if you fixate a high % extension from 50-MA due to 2020 run, you will never get a profit taking decision. 

Stock price extensions have to be accounted with price range volatility because that's a variable in a stock behavior. Only ETFs like $QQQ may apply fixed % extension because their volatility has been very stable throughout decades.
