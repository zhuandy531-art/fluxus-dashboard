## Tweet Content

I recently came across https://www.financecharts.com/ and found it to be noteworthy enough to deserve its own post. The site provides 20-year charts and financial data, including metrics, completely free and without requiring any sign-ups. For those interested in long term growth investing, there is a DCF segment that automatically assesses the fair value of the company based on the FCF and adjustable discount rate.

What I find particularly valuable is its capability to offer a quick overview of key competitors, a comparative YTD return of the top 10 peers (ie $NVDA , useful for assessing relative strength), and the symbols of ETFs (usually leveraged or inverse) associated with the stock/industry group of interest.

There is also dedicated screener for stocks/ETF on its own but there is limitation to customization based on stock price performance (correct me if i'm wrong, i'm unable to sort data via strongest 1-week, 1-month movers etc). If you're interested in leveraged ETFs, similar to how Sean Ryan and I rely on them for our trading ideas, I believe they provide the most extensive list I've come across. Moreover, the data can be easily downloaded directly to your Excel.

https://www.financecharts.com/etf-screener/category-leveraged

Definitely worth bookmarking; this website will undoubtedly become a part of my daily routine.
