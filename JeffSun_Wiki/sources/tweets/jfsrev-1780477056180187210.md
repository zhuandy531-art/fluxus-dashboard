## Tweet Content

To identify your most prolific trading strategy, you should review your own trading journal, not on opinions. Here are some deep dive you could easily explore into;

+ your entry approach, how did u identify it? can u refine it to improve performance? what are the parameters of refinement in your consideration? (market cap, avg $ vol etc, ADR% etc, removing stocks in utilities sector etc?)

+ reason for your most profitable trade (is it via tight entry equating to larger than avg position size % to account equity, or extended run,

+ optimal level (R) to take partial take profit, avg holding period of a good trade, is closing full position after closing below 10-MA optimal? explore setting LOD stop 1 day after winner close below 10-MA, will there be trajectory in nett % performance of last 50 trades?

+possibility and potential of scaling down position size of unrealized losses before SL, 

+weeding out random trades that depleted your performance, can it be avoided in future?

If you're presently on the sidelines, anticipating the market to stabilize and demonstrate its potential, this is the perfect opportunity to refine your approach and bolster your confidence in your trading style before re-entering the market. You should feel confident in taking risks in the market based on mathematical data of your own supporting your decisions, rather than relying solely on others' opinions.

'trading inevitably involves suboptimal decision-making' -@KayKlingson 13/4 newsletter
