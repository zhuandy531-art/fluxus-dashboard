## Tweet Content

The most underrated aspect of trade management and risk/reward is the effect of small losers.

Small losers dramatically affect your averages due to the fact that most strategies have the potential to win far more than they lose, even at the scalp level.

Instead of wondering how you can squeeze more water out of a rock, you can invert the process into, “how small can I lose when I’m wrong?” in order to achieve a similar result.

Example: 2:1 can become 2/.75 which is 2.67:1 adjusted RR, a massive leap.

It gets crazier at higher R like 3:.75 is 4:1 and so forth. 

Even if your average win decreases trying to lose smaller, you may very well experience a big boon in your trade expectation -> equity curve projection.

This part of the trade is ephemeral, when you’re not quite winning or losing yet.

Traders can flub trades by overemphasizing small losses and hamstring good trades needlessly, it needs to be adjusted to one’s overall strategy and serve a real purpose.

For this I would systematize it and make it so you’re prompted to lose less versus trying to wrestle with your emotions.

The majority of traders focus on winning bigger and sometimes go backwards by taking even larger losses.

Consider a 4:2 RR will be the same as 2:1. Yeah you won bigger but at what cost?

It comes down to the root, bigger =/= better.

When you can understand this and implement it with intention, you’ll be able to improve your trading in unexpected ways.

This is almost never discussed because it’s not sexy.

Without understanding this concept it will be imperceptible, you will be missing out on a golden nugget.

The effect is dramatic, but back to your regular programmed clickbait feed.
