I may not have a foolproof solution for you because it is strategy and timeframe dependent. here is what I do

1. master watchlist of various setups. all screening outputs all go into them. I usually do this screen on a weekly basis. (if u trade 2 types of setups, u should have 2 master watchlist). I have about 200+ in total at the moment. 

2. stalk watchlist, a filtered from master watchlist where the stocks in the list are in the midst of setting up within a 5 daily session timeframe. it's filtered immediately from master watchlist during the weekend, on week on week basis. I need to emphasis that I am more than willing for names in this watchlist to run without me, basically because I am keeping true to my own rules and do not want randomness in my selection for execution 

3. focus watchlist, stocks that are filtered from stalk watchlist and have all price action and volume parameters setup for upcoming immediate session. I have only 6-7 from earlier session. most time I have less than 12 and I only set price alerts on them, solely to focus on this and not deviate from the lane of my rules

I hope the above process helps in your watchlist management, and not missed out on trades.
